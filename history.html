ANCIENT EGYPT

In ancient Egypt, cats were considered divine symbols, demi-deities, with symbols and carvings of them everywhere around ancient Egyptian treasuries. Hieroglyphics, statues, patterns. Most notably, Bastet, the goddess of protection, pleasure, and the bringer of good health. She had the head of a black feline, and a female body. She was the daughter of Ra, the sun god, and it was believed that at night she would turn into a cat to protect Ra from his greatest enemy, the serpent Apep.

JAPAN

In Japan, it is believed if a single woman owns a black cat, the cat would bring her many suitors. Black Maneki Neko (common Japanese figurine) are used to ward off evil.

PIRATES

Pirates believed encountering a black cat was bad luck. Furthermore, if a black cat jumped aboard a pirate ship then jumped off, that ship would later sink.
